Sprint 2 Cultural Blog Post

My Learning Plan

23 November 2019

Goals

My long term career goal is get a job in the tech as a software developer. I would like to work for a company who’s values are aligned with mine.

In terms of human skills I would definitely like to work on public speaking, whether in large group situations or in presentations.

Strengths and Limitations

I am quite disciplined and determined. I don’t get flustered easily. Im good at planning and organising. Approachable and easy going. I am a big team player and really enjoy being around people and learning from them.

I have a tendency to set high standard for myself so I will definitely need to work on when its the right time to leave something and move on.

Workload

For myself the key to managing the EDA workload will be about having a good system in place. Taking the time out to look over the programme, break it down, assess how long things will take and scheduling in the time to do it. I enjoy using task management and calendar tools so I will definitely be utilising these to aid me. Keeping too a routine and building habits will be very important. I like having regular work hours and knowing when it’s time to finish.

Another key aspect is discussing the workload with my family and friends, making them aware that I will essentially be unavailable for social events over the next few weeks.

Support

What I would like from the facilitator team is guidance and support when needed. Regular honest feedback about what I could do differently or improve or if anything is offtrack. I am very receptive to feedback, even critical and will view it as an opportunity to learn.

In terms of asking for help I will obviously attempt to solve the problem first myself. If it’s a technical problem the next step will be asking google to see if anyone else has had the same issue. Failing that I will then consult my facilitator or the group. I do like to try and work things out for myself so I may need to get a little bit better at asking for help if something is taking too long.

Reassessing my Learning Plan for Bootcamp

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term career goal remains the same, I would like to get a job as a software developer. Im hoping that once i get more immersed in bootcamp I will be able to pinpoint which area of development I enjoy more and am more suited to.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Im a good team player and I have a lot of experience working and leading teams so I think this will definitely be one of my biggest strengths. In terms of limitations I think I will need to not get too worried about asking for help when its needed.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Some of the core material may be quite challenging. Im not really used to too much self-examination and reflection so this may be tough at first!

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like to work on my presenting skills and also communication which definitely tie in together.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Be supportive when needed. I also think giving the right kind of feedback is very important. If I need a nudge in a certain area or there is anything I can do better or improve on I would like honest feedback about it.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

My expectation is to enter bootcamp with an open mind, learn as much as I can and take as much as I can away from the experience. Help others as much as I can and not be too hard on myself.
